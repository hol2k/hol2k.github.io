Chiptune Music

Chiptune is a genre of music based on early computer hardware, notably arcade machines and game consoles.

Traits

Chiptune music is, by definition, computer-generated, and not often performed live (except e.g. in the context of a video game). Instruments in chiptune music usually consist of simple waveforms; for example, pulse waves often serve as melody and accompaniment, while triangle, sawtooth, and random noise typically feature as drums and bass. Pre-recorded sound samples may also be used, with low quality and duration to save on file space.

Composition

Modern chiptune music is often composed using tracker software, which displays music notation differently than ordinary sheet music.


A short excerpt of sheet music.

In sheet music, time progresses horizontally depending on note type, and pitch and instruments are both arranged vertically. Each instrument has its own staff, and height of notes on that staff indicates pitch.

The same music as represented by a tracker.

In a tracker, music is represented as a table instead. Time progresses vertically in fixed steps, and there are a fixed number of channels. Each cell contains information about the pitch of note to play, and which instrument to play it with; each channel can only play one instrument at a time.
